BrickMaster Open-Source Regulation Update

Over the past several months, the BrickMaster project has proudly operated as an open-source initiative. Our intention was to empower developers, encourage transparency, and allow the community to contribute to the future of BrickMaster.

Unfortunately, despite our best efforts, we have experienced repeated instances of code theft. Individuals have taken advantage of the open-source model to strip features, rebrand BrickMaster, and present stolen work as their own. These actions undermine the integrity of our team and the trust we've built with our community.

As a result, the BrickMaster Team has made the difficult decision to remove open-source compatibility moving forward. Effective immediately, the repository will no longer contain active source code updates. Only Discord Developer Compliance and official BrickMaster developers will retain direct access to the source code.

This change ensures that the project continues to grow under a secure and trusted environment, while still allowing our community to benefit from BrickMaster as a tool. We will continue to publish updates, changelogs, and documentation, but the underlying source will no longer be freely available.

We understand this decision may disappoint some contributors, but we believe this step is necessary to protect the long-term vision of BrickMaster. Our team remains committed to delivering innovative features, maintaining stability, and supporting roleplay communities worldwide.

We thank everyone who has contributed to BrickMaster during its open-source phase. Your feedback and ideas have shaped the project into what it is today.
